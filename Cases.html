Cases

Cases help provide space and safety for each of the various components a PC needs in order to be able to run. It's chasses is the main frame of the PC case that houses all of those internal componenets. The side panels allows the user quick and easy access to the interior of the case, allowing installation and maintenance of components. The front panel of the case contains various ports, buttons, and indicators for connecting peripherals, accessing USB ports, audio jacks, conroller power and reset functions. Expansion slows are openings on the rear panel that allow for expansion cards such as graphics card, sound cards, and network adapters can be installed. Drive bays are areas indie the case where storage drives like the HDD and SSDs can be mounted. Cases also come with cable management help, such as cable routing holes, tie-down points, and velcro straps.
