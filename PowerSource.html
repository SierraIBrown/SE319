PSU

6 main parts make up the power supply units. The transformer, rectifier, filtering capacitors and inductors, voltage regulator modules, power connectors, and protection circuits. The transformer converts the current coming from the outlet into the required voltage level for each component. The rectifier takes the lower-voltage AC into a direct current. The filtering capacitors and inductos then takes those pulsating DC waveforms to provide a stable and clean DC output. The power connector then takes and distributes the DC power to the other various components. The protection circuits safeguard the PSU from damage due to electrical faults or abnormalities.
