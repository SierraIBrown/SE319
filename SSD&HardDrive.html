SSD &
Hard
Drives

Solid State Drives (SSD) and Hard Drives both store long term data on your computer. There are many differences between the two, and both have their own advantages. SSDs have memory chips, controllers, cache, and interface. They provide faster data access, have no moving parts, are energy efficient, and are more compact than HDDs. HDDs have platter, read/write heads, spindle motors, and controllers. They are very cost effective, offer higher capacity, have compatibility with much older computers, and can last for several years with proper care and maintenance.
